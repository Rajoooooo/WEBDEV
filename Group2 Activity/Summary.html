Summary

In ancient Japan, a monkey-man, who earned a living by showcasing his monkey's tricks, decides to sell the old monkey to the butcher because it forgets its tricks. The monkey overhears the plan and seeks advice from a wise boar in the forest. The boar suggests a clever scheme: he will temporarily snatch the master's baby, causing a commotion, and the monkey must then rescue the child. The plan works perfectly. The boar takes the baby, causing panic, and the monkey heroically retrieves the child, earning the gratitude of the family. When the butcher arrives, the family decides not to sell the monkey, and he lives peacefully for the rest of his days, no longer subjected to mistreatment.

Moral Lesson

The tale of the sagacious monkey teaches us the importance of valuing and respecting those who have served us faithfully, even as they age or face challenges. It emphasizes the significance of wisdom and cleverness in overcoming adversity. Additionally, the story underscores the idea that empathy and compassion can lead to unexpected solutions and positive outcomes. Ultimately, the moral lesson encourages us to recognize the worth of others, appreciate their contributions, and find humane alternatives to harsh judgments or actions.
